Jose Rizal
José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda, commonly known as José Rizal, was a Filipino nationalist, polymath, and the national hero of the Philippines. He was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines, and was executed on December 30, 1896, in Manila, Philippines, at the age of 35.
Juan Luna
Juan Luna de San Pedro y Novicio Ancheta was a Filipino painter, sculptor and a political activist of the Philippine Revolution during the late 19th century. He became one of the first recognized Philippine artists.
Andres Bonifacio
Andres Bonifacio (1863-1897), was the creator and head of the nationalist Katipunan organisation, who led the revolution against the Spanish in August 1896.
Teresa Magbanua
Teresa Magbanua y Ferraris, better known as Teresa Magbanua and dubbed as the "Visayan Joan of Arc", was a Filipino schoolteacher and military leader
Emilio Aguinaldo
Emilio Aguinaldo y Famy QSC CCLH PMM KGCR was a Filipino revolutionary, statesman, and military leader who is the youngest President of the Philippines and became the first President of the Philippines and of an Asian constitutional republic.
Muhammad Dipatuan Kudarat
Muhammad Dipatuan Kudarat was the 7th Sultan of Maguindanao from 1619 to 1671. He was a direct descendant of Shariff Kabungsuwan, a Malay-Arab noble from Johor who brought Islam to Mindanao between the 13th and 14th centuries..
